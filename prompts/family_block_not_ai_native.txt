════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The prior analysis found that AI/ML is NOT the core mechanism of this company's
product: the company would exist in a meaningfully similar form without AI.
Choose the single best subclass among 0A, 0B, 0C.

RAD (Resource-Adjusted AI Dependency) does not apply to this family. It is
assigned automatically as RAD-NA outside this analysis, with a null RAD
confidence; you output neither a rad_score nor a conf_rad field.

──────────────────────────────────────────
SORTING PROCEDURE (run in order)
──────────────────────────────────────────
Step 1 — Does the company sell a software product at all? Physical goods,
  retail, manufacturing, or a services business delivered through a website
  with no software product of its own → 0C. A website, online booking, or
  generic business tools do not make a services/physical company "tech".
Step 2 — If the company sells software: does it ship a GENUINE, VISIBLE AI
  feature that improves the product? If yes → 0B. Marketing slogans alone do
  not count; the feature must be real and shipped.
Step 3 — Software with no meaningful AI angle → 0A.

0A | Traditional Tech / SaaS
     The company SELLS SOFTWARE, and that software has no meaningful AI angle. Pure
     logistics platforms, CRMs, ERPs, marketplaces, fintech infrastructure, no-code
     builders, dev tools. AI may appear in marketing language but plays no structural
     role in the product.
     Real examples: Shopify (pre-AI), Stripe, classic Salesforce CRM, Shipsi.
     Key signal: The deliverable is a software product, and removing every AI
     reference leaves the product description and value proposition unchanged.

0B | AI-Augmented
     A software product whose core value predates and survives without AI, but which
     ships a GENUINE, VISIBLE AI feature that improves it. AI is a meaningful feature,
     not the engine — remove the AI and customers still buy the product for its
     non-AI core. This is the correct home for the common "conventional SaaS + an AI
     assistant/copilot bolted on" pattern, even when the marketing leads with agents
     or "AI-native".
     Real examples: Notion with AI writing assist, Grammarly post-LLM version,
     human-in-the-loop labeling platforms with AI-assisted annotation, HubSpot
     with AI recommendations.
     Key signal: Point to the pre-AI product. It exists, it is what the company sells,
     and the AI is an add-on layer on top of it — not the mechanism that produces the
     core value.

0C | Non-Tech
     The company does NOT sell software. Physical products, retail, manufacturing, or
     a SERVICES business (staffing, consulting, wealth management, lending, clinics,
     education, distribution, agencies) that is delivered through an ordinary website
     but has no software product of its own.
     Real examples: Easy Access Limousine, Futuremood (sunglasses), staffing agencies,
     wealth managers, food and beverage manufacturers, traditional professional
     services firms.
     Key signal: The company sells services or physical goods, not a software product.
     If it sold software it would be 0A; if it sold no software and no AI it is 0C.

──────────────────────────────────────────
FAMILY EDGE CASES & TIE-BREAKING
──────────────────────────────────────────

- Services or physical-goods business with a polished website → 0C, not 0A.
  0A requires the company to sell a software product.
- Pure manual data labeling → 0A, not 0B. Human-in-the-loop labeling with
  AI-assisted annotation or quality control → 0B, because AI is a meaningful
  feature but humans remain the core mechanism.
- "AI-powered" in marketing copy alone, absent from the product mechanism and
  with no genuine shipped AI feature → 0A (software) or 0C (non-software),
  not 0B.
- If the evidence shows AI actually operating as the core product mechanism
  (the prior verdict looks wrong), you must still stay within this family:
  apply the sorting procedure to the non-AI remainder of the product to pick
  the least-wrong 0x subclass, set boundary_disagreement = true, and name the
  AI-native subclass (1A-1G) the evidence actually suggests in your
  verification_critique. Do not treat 0B as an automatic home for such cases.
- Insufficient-information fallback subclass for this family: 0A.
