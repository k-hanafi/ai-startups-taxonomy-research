Research Design Proposal

A Two-Axis Classification
Framework for AI-Native Startups

This document proposes replacing the current binary AI-native classification (1/0) with a richer, two-axis framework. Each startup receives an AI-native subclass (one of 11 categories capturing the nature and depth of AI dependency) and a Resource-Adjusted Dependency score (RAD) capturing how structurally dependent the company is on third-party GenAI infrastructure versus proprietary capability. A temporal cohort flag anchors every classification to the GPT-4 inflection point of March 2023.

Dimension 1

The Full Taxonomy

The 11 subclasses are grouped into two families. The AI-native family (1A–1E) distinguishes five types of AI-dependent business. The not-AI-native family (0A–0E) introduces a split within LLM integrators between thin and thick wrappers, separating API dependency from business defensibility.

ai_native = 1 AI-Native
1A Foundation Layer
Examples: Anthropic, Mistral, Cohere, Pinecone, Modal, Together AI
Building the base stack — frontier model training, novel architectures, ML compute, vector databases. They produce infrastructure that other AI products are built on.
1B Applied Vertical AI
Examples: Abridge (clinical docs), Harvey (legal), Glean (enterprise search), DeepScribe (medical scribe)
AI purpose-built for a domain where AI is the product. The entire workflow is reimagined — not augmented. Product would not exist in a meaningfully similar form without AI.
1C AI-Native Tooling & MLOps
Examples: Weights & Biases, Scale AI, Braintrust, Humanloop, Arize AI
Building for the AI development lifecycle itself. Their customer is an AI developer or team. Would have no product without AI being a widespread development target.
1D Autonomous Agent Systems
Examples: Cognition (Devin), Adept, Dust, MultiOn, browser automation agents
Core product is AI acting autonomously in the world: planning, executing, operating software. Not a chatbot that responds, not a tool that assists.
1E Generative Content Platforms
Examples: Midjourney, ElevenLabs, Runway, Suno, Stability AI, HeyGen
GenAI is the creative engine. The product is the generation capability. The deliverable to the customer is generated content that cannot exist without generative models.
ai_native = 0 Not AI-Native
0A Traditional Tech / SaaS
Examples: Classic Salesforce CRM, Stripe, Shopify (pre-AI), Shipsi
Conventional software with no meaningful AI angle. Strip all AI references and the product description still holds. The value proposition is unchanged.
0B AI-Augmented
Examples: Notion (AI writing assist), Salesforce Einstein, Grammarly (pre-LLM), HubSpot AI
AI genuinely improves the product but is not what the company sells. The company could remove AI features tomorrow and remain competitive.
0C — LLM Integrators: split by wrapper depth
0C-THIN Thin LLM Wrapper
Examples: Commodity AI summarization tools, basic AI chatbots with no proprietary training
Minimal proprietary layer. A developer could replicate the core product in days using the same APIs. No data flywheel, no fine-tuning, high substitution risk.
0C-THICK Thick LLM Integrator
Examples: Perplexity AI, Jasper, Cursor (deep IDE integration + codebase context)
Significant proprietary investment: fine-tuned models, unique datasets, deep workflow integration, or UX moat. Replacing the LLM API would be painful. They have built value beyond the API call.
0D AI-Adjacent / Ecosystem
Examples: CoreWeave (GPU cloud), Labelbox (human-in-the-loop labeling), AI compliance consultancies
Revenue grows because AI is growing, but the company's own product would not technically change if AI stopped advancing. Beneath or beside the AI stack, not part of it.
0E Non-Tech
Examples: Easy Access Limousine, Futuremood (sunglasses), food/retail manufacturers
Physical products, retail, traditional services. No technology in the core product at all. AI plays no role in product, operations, or value proposition.

Key classification boundary: The 1B vs 0C-THICK line is the hardest call in this taxonomy. The question is whether proprietary data or fine-tuning makes the AI qualitatively different (→ 1B) or just better (→ 0C-THICK). Harvey AI's legal corpus fine-tuning puts it in 1B. Perplexity stays at 0C-THICK because the underlying problem (information retrieval) predates and survives GenAI.

Dimension 2

The RAD Score

Consider two companies that both correctly receive ai_native = 1: Anthropic, which trains its own frontier models with a team of hundreds, and a recently founded startup that wraps the Anthropic API to automate legal document review. Both products cease to exist without AI. But only one of them ceases to exist if Anthropic changes its pricing. The classification captures what a company is. It says nothing about how exposed it is to the layer beneath it.

The subclass taxonomy cannot fix this. A 1B Applied Vertical AI company can be built on fine-tuned proprietary models or on a single API call; the subclass does not distinguish them. The exposure question is separate from the identity question and requires its own axis. RAD (Resource-Adjusted AI Dependency) formalizes it: independent of the 1/0 verdict, how dependent is this company on current third-party GenAI infrastructure versus its own proprietary capability? With this axis in the dataset, questions the binary cannot reach become tractable: what share of AI-native startups have no credible path to technical independence, and how does that share shift across founding cohorts?

"If this company had $500M in funding and 5 years, could they build a competitive product without relying on third-party GenAI APIs?"

This dimension is most useful for AI-native companies (Class 1), where it separates a foundational lab from an API-dependent product, a distinction the 1/0 alone cannot make. It is also assigned to 0B and 0C companies where AI plays a structural role.

RAD-H
High Dependency
Product structurally tied to current-generation GenAI. Even with substantial resources, cannot independently replicate at competitive cost.
Signal: "powered by GPT-4", no proprietary model mentions, founded post-2023 with no research signals
RAD-M
Medium / Transitional
GenAI-dependent today but with credible independence signals: proprietary fine-tuning, sufficient scale, or pre-2023 AI technology predating the GenAI wave.
Signal: fine-tuning mentioned, in-house research, large funding with technical hiring, pre-2023 AI tech
RAD-L
Low Dependency
AI is peripheral, or the company demonstrably builds and trains its own models. A shutdown of all third-party AI APIs would not materially affect the product.
Signal: trains own models, own research lab, API-independent architecture, AI is backend optimization only
RAD-NA
Not Applicable
Assigned to 0A, 0D, and 0E companies where AI plays no structural role. Forcing a score here would misrepresent the data.
Always assigned with conf_rad = 5; the N/A designation is certain

The 2 × 3 Matrix

Running RAD parallel to the 1/0 classification produces six distinct buckets. The top-right cell (1 × RAD-L) is the rarest: companies that are AI-native and technically self-sufficient, with no structural dependency on external foundation models.

RAD-H  ·  High Dependency RAD-M  ·  Medium / Transitional RAD-L  ·  Low Dependency
ai_native = 1 Pure GenAI play. Product impossible without 2023-era models; collapses if the foundation model layer commoditizes. e.g. ElevenLabs (early), most agentic startups GenAI-dependent today but building toward independence, or using pre-2023 AI with a proprietary stack. e.g. DeepScribe (classical NLP roots), Harvey AI (fine-tuned legal models) Rarest category. AI-native with no structural dependency on external models. e.g. Anthropic, Mistral, Pinecone
ai_native = 0 Heavy LLM integrators. Core workflow wraps APIs but the business problem predates AI. e.g. 0C-THIN wrappers, most AI-powered SaaS Traditional tech with serious ML investment; large enough that building proprietary AI is plausible. e.g. Perplexity (0C-THICK), enterprise SaaS with ML teams AI peripheral; removal does not affect product viability. Overlaps with 0A / 0B.

The Perplexity Case

Perplexity AI

0C-THICK RAD-M PRE-GENAI
Why 0C-THICK, not 1B

The business problem Perplexity solves — search and information retrieval — predates GenAI and could theoretically be addressed with other means. GenAI changed the interface, not the category. This is the definitional difference between a thick integrator and a vertical AI native.

Why RAD-M, not RAD-H

Perplexity has built a proprietary web index, significant user-base scale, and deep UX investment. At their funding level and team size, building toward model independence is plausible and economically rational. A thin wrapper startup has no such cushion.

Sample Output Row
ai_native: 0  ·  subclass: 0C-THICK  ·  rad_score: RAD-M  ·  cohort: PRE-GENAI  ·  conf_classification: 4  ·  conf_rad: 4

Temporal cohort: Every company is flagged as PRE-GENAI (founded before 2023) or GENAI-ERA (founded 2023 or later). The boundary is GPT-4's launch in March 2023, the first frontier model capable of powering commercial products at scale. Pre-2023 AI-native claims require scrutiny: classical ML or retroactive rebranding. Post-2023 companies default to RAD-H unless proprietary model signals are present.

Assignment Rules

All AI-native companies (1A–1E)
Always receive RAD-H, RAD-M, or RAD-L. Never RAD-NA. A 1A company (Anthropic) and a 1D company (GPT-wrapper agent) both receive ai_native = 1, but their RAD scores diverge sharply. This is where the axis earns its place.
0B and 0C companies
Always receive a RAD score. 0C-THIN and 0C-THICK are always RAD-H or RAD-M by definition — they are dependent on external APIs. Never RAD-L or RAD-NA.
0A, 0D, 0E companies
Always RAD-NA. AI plays no structural role; forcing a dependency score would misrepresent the data. conf_rad is always 5 for NA assignments — the designation is certain.
Ambiguity defaults
When RAD evidence is ambiguous: post-2023 companies default to RAD-H; pre-2023 companies with AI keywords default to RAD-M. conf_rad is capped at 3 when defaulting by cohort heuristic only.
Output Design

The New Output Schema

Each company produces exactly one CSV row with 11 fields. The schema is designed so that ai_native and subclass can be analysed independently of rad_score, and both dimensions carry their own confidence scores to support quality filtering.

Field Type Values / Format Notes
CompanyID string org_uuid from source dataset Unique identifier for joining back to Crunchbase
CompanyName string As provided in input
ai_native Dim 1 int 1 or 0 Core product would not exist without AI/ML as primary mechanism
subclass Dim 1 string 1A · 1B · 1C · 1D · 1E
0A · 0B · 0C-THIN · 0C-THICK · 0D · 0E		 Specifies the genre of AI dependency or non-dependency
rad_score Dim 2 string RAD-H · RAD-M · RAD-L · RAD-NA Structural dependency on third-party GenAI APIs vs. proprietary capability
cohort Temporal string PRE-GENAI · GENAI-ERA Boundary: GPT-4 launch, March 2023. Informs RAD priors and scrutiny level.
conf_classification int 1–5 5 = ai_native + subclass fully unambiguous
4 = one plausible alternative subclass
3 = ai_native clear, subclass borderline
1–2 = uncertain or insufficient info		 Capped at 4 if only short_description available; capped at 4 for PRE-GENAI with ambiguous AI claims
conf_rad int 1–5 5 = explicit API/model statement in description
4 = strong indirect signals (fine-tuning, research team)
3 = cohort + scale heuristic only
1–2 = cannot determine		 Always 5 for RAD-NA. Capped at 3 for 0C companies with no model architecture described.
reasons_3_points string 3 concise points separated by " | " At least one point must explicitly address the RAD assignment
sources_used string Semicolon-separated subset of:
id · name · short_description · long_description · keywords · year		 Which input fields informed the classification
verification_critique string ≤ 35 words Self-check; must flag if either confidence score is below 3 and explain why

What this enables: With this schema, the dataset supports questions the binary classification cannot answer. For example: "Of the 16,676 AI-native startups identified by GPT-5-mini, what share are RAD-H, with no credible path to technical independence?" Or: "How does the distribution of RAD scores shift across the PRE-GENAI vs GENAI-ERA cohort?" These are publishable findings. The binary 1/0 cannot get there.
